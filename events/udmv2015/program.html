UDMV 2015

3rd Eurographics Workshop on Urban Data Modelling and Visualisation
November 23rd, 2015 · Delft, the Netherlands







Program

This is a preliminary schedule and it is subject to changes. The final program will be published a week before the event.
The workshop is a one-day event on Monday 23 November 2015.

Accepted papers


  1. Who are my visitors and where do they come from? An analysis based on Foursquare check-ins and place- based semantics
    Pierre Hallot, Kathleen Stewart, and Roland Billen
    University of Liège (Belgium)

  2. Dynamizers - Modeling and implementing dynamic properties for semantic 3D city models
    Kanishk Chaturvedi and Thomas H. Kolbe
    TU Munich (Germany)

  3. Conceptualizing, Managing and Developing: A Web Based 3D City Information Model for Urban Energy Demand Simulation
    Rushikesh Padsala and Volker Coors
    HFT Stuttgart (Germany)

  4. Far-LoD: Level of Detail for Sky View Factor calculation in Large Cities
    David Muñoz Novoa, Benoit Beckers, Gonzalo Besuievsky, and Gustavo Patow
    University of Girona (Spain)

  5. Commercial Evolution Simulation
    Carlos Soriano and Gustavo Patow
    University of Girona (Spain)

  6. Visibility analysis in a point cloud based on the medial axis transform
    Ravi Peters, Hugo Ledoux, and Filip Biljecki
    TU Delft (Netherlands)

  7. 5D Multi-Purpose Land Information System
    Charalabos Ioannidis, Styliani Verykokou, Sofia Soile, and Potsiou Chryssy
    National Technical University of Athens (Greece)

  8. On the acquisition of human emotions in space and time
    Julius Schöning and Corinna E. Bonhage
    University of Osnabrück (Germany)

  9. Combining Geometry, Topology and Semantics for Generic Building Description and Simulations
    Sébastien Horna, Guillaume Damiand, Abdoulaye Diakité, and Daniel Meneveaux
    University of Poitiers (France) and University of Lyon (France)

  10. Automatic semantic-preserving conversion between OBJ and CityGML
    Filip Biljecki and Ken Arroyo Ohori
    TU Delft (Netherlands)

  11. Representing urban phenomena in their context and at different LoD: from raw data to appropriate LoD
    Ha Pham, Anne Ruas, and T. Libourel
    IFSTTAR (France)

Presentations

  • Prof Jantien Stoter (3D geoinformation, TU Delft): Urban modelling in the Netherlands
  • Prof Elmar Eisemann (Computer graphics, TU Delft): Harvesting 4D Worlds
